Number of Bikeshare Stations vs. Number of Traffic Incidents

In the City of Chicago between 2013-2017

Group Members: Aizza, Mehreen, Nikki and Rommel

Aim: To vizualize the relationship (if any) between the increase of bikeshare stations in Chicago and traffic incidents in the city.

Methods:Heatmap, Piechart, timeseries line chart.
Findings:

We first plotted the stations and bike violations on a map together, but this did not show a very clear correlation.

Next we plotted the same data on a line chart, scaling for the 2 different data sets. And this shows a clear negative correlation between the bike share trips taken and speed violations.

This might not prove causation since there could be an external factor driving both these changes, it is interesting to see the values move together.



It was also interesting to note that only around 25% of rides were taken by females.



Furthermore, its interesting to see how the number of subscribers has increased every year, while the number of customers remains the same from year to year, showing that the brand is growing its repeat customers day by day.
